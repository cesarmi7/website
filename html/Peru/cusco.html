Cusco City!

While Cusco is most famously associated with being the beginning of the trail to the renowned Machu Picchu, it is also a location worth seeing and appreciating on its own. Nestled high in the Andes, this historic city showcases Peru's ancient Incan traditions, modern-day Quechua culture, and colonial Spanish architecture.

How to get to Cusco depends on your preference; personally, arriving by plane is preferable as it is only an hour's journey from Lima compared to 15 - 20 hours by bus. From Cusco airport to the Plaza de Armas (center of the city) is not very far, but I would suggest taking a taxi due to the altitude issue. However, for cheap taxis, I recommend downloading the Didi app.

At an altitude of 3,399 meters, it's often the city where most people acclimatize before embarking on the Inca Trail. While I recommend acclimatizing in the lower altitude cities within the Sacred Valley, Cusco is still a great option to spend a few days preparing the body for what lies ahead. I also recommend trying coca tea, or taking a pill to prevent altitude sickness.

During my stay in Cusco, I chose to stay at

Kokopelli Hostel Cusco

,

which offered a unique and charming atmosphere.

Like most hostels, it is perfect for solo travelers or backpackers looking to explore Cusco on a budget. It's conveniently located close to all the must-see attractions in Cusco, and the staff is extremely friendly and knowledgeable about the area.

I loved staying here and meeting some amazing people during my stay. The common area is great for meeting new people, and the rooftop always has events going on to join. I was able to join two different tours there and take a cooking class at their hostel.
